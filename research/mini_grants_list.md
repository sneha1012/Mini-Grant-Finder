# Relevant Mini-Grants for Delta Rising Foundation

*Last updated: February 16, 2026*

This document catalogs mini-grant opportunities relevant to Delta Rising Foundation's programs and mission. Grants are organized by relevance to Delta's four core programs and cross-cutting themes.

---

## Priority Legend
- **HIGH** — Strong alignment with Delta's mission + currently accepting applications
- **MEDIUM** — Good alignment but deadline approaching or geographic constraints
- **LOW** — Partial alignment or limited funding

---

## 1. Climate & Environmental Justice Grants

### Portland Clean Energy Fund (PCEF) Mini Grants
- **Amount:** Up to $5,000 per application
- **Deadline:** Quarterly cycles; next cycle resumes **April 2026** (Jan 2026 on hiatus)
- **Total Fund:** $200,000/year ($50,000/quarter)
- **Focus:** Climate action + social justice
- **Relevance:** HIGH — Directly aligns with climate + equity mission
- **Note:** Additional $15,000/cycle for 82nd Ave Corridor projects
- **URL:** https://www.portland.gov/bps/cleanenergy/mini-grants

### EPA Environmental & Climate Justice Community Change Grants
- **Amount:** Varies (large program)
- **Deadline:** Rolling basis
- **Focus:** Climate adaptation, resilience, pollution reduction, workforce development in disadvantaged communities
- **Eligibility:** Partnerships between nonprofits and government/academic institutions
- **Relevance:** HIGH — Matches multiple Delta programs
- **URL:** https://www.epa.gov/resilient-investments/environmental-and-climate-justice-community-change-grants-program

### Climate Justice Microgrants (Climate Advocacy Lab)
- **Amount:** Varies
- **Deadline:** Check website for current cycle
- **Focus:** U.S. climate justice organizations documenting lessons learned and best practices
- **Deliverables:** Case studies, reports, toolkits highlighting community organizing wins
- **Relevance:** MEDIUM — Good for knowledge-sharing component
- **URL:** https://climateadvocacylab.org/climate-justice-microgrants

### Texas Grassroots Mini Grants
- **Amount:** Up to $4,000
- **Deadline:** Rolling (no deadlines!)
- **Focus:** Grassroots environmental groups, organizing, direct action, research, capacity building
- **Eligibility:** Prioritizes groups with budgets under $50,000; informal orgs also eligible
- **Relevance:** MEDIUM — Good if Delta has Texas-based activities
- **URL:** https://www.txenvironment.org/mini-grants

## 2. Urban Greening / Tree Canopy / Shade Grants

*Relevant to: Resilience Nursery & More Shade for More People*

### Chesapeake Bay Trust — Urban Trees Mini Grant
- **Amount:** Up to $10,000
- **Deadline:** Check website for current cycle
- **Focus:** Small tree planting projects in urban, underserved areas (Maryland)
- **Eligibility:** Nonprofits, community associations, faith-based organizations
- **Match:** Encouraged but not required
- **Relevance:** HIGH — Direct fit for nursery + shade programs (if operating in MD)
- **URL:** https://cbtrust.org/grants/urban-trees-mini-grant-program/

### Arbor Day Foundation — Community Roots Program
- **Amount:** Varies (Inflation Reduction Act funded — $1.5B nationally)
- **Deadline:** Periodic application windows
- **Focus:** Food forests, native tree distribution, neighborhood plantings in underserved communities
- **Relevance:** HIGH — Perfect alignment with Resilience Nursery
- **URL:** https://arborday.org/perspectives/empowering-communities-fostering-urban-forests

### City of Shoreline Environmental Mini Grants
- **Amount:** Up to $5,000
- **Deadline:** January 31, 2026 (may have passed — check for next cycle)
- **Focus:** Climate preparedness, waste reduction, habitat protection, fossil fuel reduction
- **Match:** At least 20% matching funds required
- **Relevance:** MEDIUM — Good fit but geographically limited to Shoreline, WA
- **URL:** https://www.shorelinewa.gov/i-want-to/apply-for/environmental-mini-grants

## 3. Climate Resilience & Adaptation Grants

### Climate Smart Communities Initiative (CSCI) 2026
- **Amount:** $75,000 – $115,000 (larger grant!)
- **Deadline:** **March 12, 2026** ⚠️ UPCOMING
- **Total Fund:** $1.5–$2 million (16–20 awards expected)
- **Focus:** Climate adaptation projects (not mitigation)
- **Requirements:** Three required partners — climate adaptation practitioner, CBO, and local/regional government
- **Population limit:** Communities under 300,000 (or 500,000 for regional)
- **Relevance:** HIGH — Excellent fit, but requires partnership structure
- **URL:** https://climatesmartcommunity.org/funding/

## 4. Indigenous Communities & Carbon Markets

*Relevant to: Community Biodiversity Energy Carbon Network*

### Tribal Nature-Based Solutions Climate Bond 2026 (California)
- **Amount:** ~$9.2 million total program
- **Deadline:** Opens **February 13, 2026**; Step 1 due **April 15, 2026** ⚠️ UPCOMING
- **Focus:** Tribal multi-benefit nature-based solutions — land acquisition, conservation easements, water rights
- **Eligibility:** California Native American tribes and tribally-led nonprofits
- **Relevance:** HIGH — If Delta partners with tribal organizations in CA
- **URL:** https://www.grants.ca.gov/grants/tribal-nature-based-solutions-climate-bond-2026-solicitation/

### Dedicated Grant Mechanism (DGM) — Climate Investment Funds
- **Amount:** From $70M (Forest Investment Program) + $40M (Nature, People & Climate)
- **Deadline:** Varies by country/region
- **Focus:** Indigenous Peoples & Local Communities — sustainable natural resource management, land restoration, climate action using traditional knowledge
- **Relevance:** HIGH — Strong fit for the CBECN program
- **URL:** https://www.cif.org/cif-funding/dedicated-grant-mechanism

### Kawari Fund
- **Amount:** Varies
- **Focus:** Grants to Indigenous Peoples & Local Communities for rights in forest carbon finance
- **Relevance:** MEDIUM — Good for carbon market advocacy component
- **URL:** https://www.kawari.earth/
